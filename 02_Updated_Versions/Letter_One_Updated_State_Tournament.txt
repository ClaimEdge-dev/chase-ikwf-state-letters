A LETTER FOR CHASE — THIS IS YOUR TIME (Updated for State Tournament)

Chase,

I started writing this mid-season, Buddy Boy. The night I first saw it — really saw it — in your eyes. That look. That fire. That thing that separates kids who wrestle from kids who ARE wrestlers.

I went home that night and started typing. I kept rewriting it, week after week, because every time I thought I had it right, you went and did something else that proved me wrong. You kept leveling up. So I kept telling you instead of finishing the letter. Every car ride. Every practice. Every moment between us — I told you.

And you listened. Not with your ears. With your heart.

Now here we are. The night before State. And I'm finishing this letter because you already lived every word I was trying to write.

THE PRAYER — YOUR TEAM

You are never alone on that mat, Chase. You have a team that no one else can see.

Guardian angels — spiritual warriors assigned by God to protect you, to guide you, to give you strength when yours runs out. They flank you on both sides. They have your back. They are God's warriors, and they do not lose.

And right there with them — your Great-Grandma Paulette. She passed away, Buddy Boy, but she never left you. She is watching over you right now. She sees you. She knows your heart. She's been praying for this moment since before you were born. When you feel that extra warmth, that calm in the storm — that's her. That's her love wrapping around you like a hug from heaven.

Call upon Jesus. Whisper His name. "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13. That is not just a verse. That is a promise. That is a weapon. Every time you step on that mat, you walk with the King of Kings.

Mom and Dad are right here. Mom was right there with me, bleeding the same way, sacrificing the same way, believing the same way. We are your corner. Always.

All your grandparents helped get you here — Grandma Debbie, Grandpa Bob, Grandma Denise, Grandpa Tommy, Grandma Kim. Every one of them has poured into you.

Your brother Jayden looks up to you more than you know. Your sister Nova — she's your biggest little fan. Uncle Bobby, Uncle Brandon, Aunt Kelsey, Aunt Kassidy, Uncle Tyler — this is a family thing, Buddy Boy.

ROCKY VS DRAGO — YOU KNOW WHO YOU ARE

Remember Rocky IV? Rocky was the underdog. Drago was the machine — bigger, stronger, "better" on paper. But Rocky had heart. Rocky had something Drago couldn't measure.

Chase, you are Rocky. The #1, #2, #3 seeds — they might look like Drago on paper. But they don't have YOUR heart. They didn't wake up at 5am with you. They didn't run those sprints. They didn't push through when their body said stop.

You did. And that makes you dangerous.

EMINEM — LOSE YOURSELF

"You better lose yourself in the music, the moment — you own it, you better never let it go. You only get one shot, do not miss your chance to blow."

Chase, this IS that moment. Own it. Be present. Every match. Every period. Every second. Do not leave anything in the tank.

THIS IS YOUR TIME

This is your moment, Chase. This is your time. The Fabolous song — "My Time." Play it. Feel it.

You are the #4 seed at IKWF State. Out of every kid in Illinois — YOU are ranked 4th. But seeding is just a number. What matters is who shows up. Who has the heart. Who has been preparing for THIS moment.

That's you.

THE JOB AIN'T FINISHED

"The job ain't finished." — Kobe Bryant

You didn't come this far to come this far, Chase. You didn't grind all season just to show up. You did it to WIN.

But here's the truth: you already won. The kid who started this season is not the same kid reading this. You grew. You evolved. You became a wrestler. A competitor. A young man who stares pressure in the face and smiles.

So go finish the job. For YOU. Because you deserve to see what happens when preparation meets opportunity.

FINAL WORDS

I am proud of you, Chase. I am proud of WHO you are — kind, loyal, loving, hard-working, faithful. Those things matter more than any trophy. But tomorrow? Go get the trophy too.

This is your time, Buddy Boy.

I love you.

— Dad
