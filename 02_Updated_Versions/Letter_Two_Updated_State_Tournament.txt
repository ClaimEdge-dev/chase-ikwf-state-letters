THE JOB AIN'T FINISHED (Updated for State Tournament)

Chase,

"The job ain't finished." — Kobe Bryant

He said it after Game 3 of the 2009 Finals, when his team was up 2-1 and everyone else was celebrating. Kobe looked at the camera like everyone was crazy. "The job ain't finished. What's there to be happy about?"

That's you right now, Buddy Boy. You made it to State. You're the #4 seed. Everyone is impressed. Everyone is patting you on the back. Feel proud — for a minute. Then get back to work.

Because the job ain't finished.

You don't get a seed. You get a title. You get a memory that lasts forever. You get to walk into middle school next year — and high school after that — and know that you are a STATE QUALIFIER. A STATE PLACER! A STATE CHAMPION!

But that's earned. Right here. Right now.

Every kid you wrestle wants it just as bad as you. Every single one of them has a dad writing them a letter. Every single one of them has a dream. 

So what makes you different?

Your heart. Your preparation. Your team — seen and unseen.

Your Great-Grandma Paulette is watching over you. Your guardian angels flank you. Jesus walks with you. "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13. Say it. Believe it. Live it.

Mom and Dad are in your corner. We bled for this. We sacrificed. We believed. All your grandparents — Grandma Debbie, Grandpa Bob, Grandma Denise, Grandpa Tommy, Grandma Kim — they all poured into you. Jayden, Nova, your aunts and uncles — we're all here.

Go finish the job, Chase. Not for us. For YOU.

I love you, Buddy Boy.

— Dad
