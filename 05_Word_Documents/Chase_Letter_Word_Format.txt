A LETTER FOR CHASE — THIS IS YOUR TIME
IKWF State Tournament 2026 | #4 Seed


Dear Chase,

I started writing this mid-season, Buddy Boy. The night I first saw it — really saw it — in your eyes. That look. That fire. That thing that separates kids who wrestle from kids who ARE wrestlers.

I went home that night and started typing. I kept rewriting it, week after week, because every time I thought I had it right, you went and did something else that proved me wrong. You kept leveling up. So I stopped trying to write it and started TELLING you instead. Every car ride. Every practice. Every moment between us — I told you.

And you listened. Not with your ears. With your heart.

Now here we are. The night before State. And I'm finishing this letter because you already lived every word I was trying to write.


THE PRAYER — YOUR TEAM

You are never alone on that mat, Chase. You have a team that no one else can see.

Your guardian angels — spiritual warriors assigned by God to protect you, to guide you, to give you strength when yours runs out. They flank you on both sides. They have your back. They are God's warriors, and they do not lose.

And right there with them — your Great-Grandma Paulette. She passed away before you got to know her like we wish you could have, Buddy Boy, but she never left you. She is watching over you right now. She sees you. She knows your heart. She's been praying for this moment since before you were born. When you feel that extra warmth, that calm in the storm — that's her. That's her love wrapping around you like a hug from heaven.

Call upon Jesus. Whisper His name. "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13. That is not just a verse. That is a promise. That is a weapon. Every time you step on that mat, you walk with the King of Kings. The God who created the universe — He chose YOU for this moment. He equipped you. He anointed you.

Mom and Dad are right here. Mom was right there with me, bleeding the same way, sacrificing the same way, believing the same way. We are your corner. Always.

All your grandparents helped get you here — Grandma Debbie, Grandpa Bob, Grandma Denise, Grandpa Tommy, Grandma Kim. Every one of them has poured into you.

Your brother Jayden looks up to you more than you know. Your sister Nova — she's your biggest little fan. Uncle Bobby, Uncle Brandon, Aunt Kelsey, Aunt Kassidy, Uncle Tyler — this is a family thing, Buddy Boy. This is what we do. We show up for each other.


THE CHAMPIONSHIP MINDSET

Muhammad Ali said: "I am the greatest. I said that even before I knew I was."

Chase, say it. Believe it. Not from arrogance — from preparation. You are the greatest BECAUSE you have outworked everyone. Your preparation is your confidence. Your preparation is your swagger. When Ali said that, he wasn't being cocky — he was being HONEST. He put in the work when no one was watching. That's you. You put in the work. Now own it.

Michael Jordan: "I've missed more than 9,000 shots in my career. I've lost almost 300 games. Twenty-six times I've been trusted to take the game-winning shot and missed. I've failed over and over and over again in my life. And that is why I succeed."

Jordan failed publicly. He failed famously. And he kept shooting. Chase, if something doesn't go your way — you keep shooting. You keep attacking. You keep believing. Failure is not the opposite of success. It's part of it.

Kobe Bryant: "Everything negative — pressure, challenges — is all an opportunity for me to rise."

Pressure is a privilege, Buddy Boy. It means you're exactly where you're supposed to be. The fact that you FEEL this moment means you belong in it.

Tom Brady: "Too often in life, something happens and we blame other people for us not being happy or satisfied or fulfilled. So the point is, we all have choices, and we make the choice to accept people or situations or to not accept situations."

You control your response. You control your effort. You control your attitude. That's it. That's all you need.

Dan Gable: "Once you've wrestled, everything else in life is easy."

Chase, you have already done the hard part. You made it to State. You earned the #4 seed. Now you just go be you.


ROCKY VS DRAGO — YOU KNOW WHO YOU ARE

Remember when we watched Rocky IV? Rocky was the underdog. Drago was the machine — bigger, stronger, "better" on paper. The Soviets had science. Technology. Steroids. They measured every metric. Drago punched so hard he killed Apollo Creed in the ring.

But Rocky had heart. Rocky had something Drago couldn't measure. Something money and science and steroids couldn't buy. Rocky had the eye of the tiger. He had the will to keep getting up when everyone else would have stayed down.

And in the end? Heart won. Because heart is the one thing you can't teach. You can't buy it. You can't fake it.

Chase, you are Rocky. The #1 seed, the #2 seed, the #3 seed — they might look like Drago on paper. They might have the bigger name, the bigger club, the bigger reputation. But they don't have YOUR heart. They didn't wake up at 5am with you. They didn't run those sprints when their legs were burning. They didn't push through when their body said stop and their mind said "just this once, take it easy."

You did.

You made those choices. You did the work when no one was watching. You suffered when it would have been easier to quit. And that — THAT — is what makes you dangerous, Buddy Boy. That is what makes you someone they should be worried about.

They have the seeding. You have the heart.

In wrestling, heart wins.


EMINEM — LOSE YOURSELF

"Look, if you had one shot, or one opportunity
To seize everything you ever wanted, in one moment
Would you capture it, or just let it slip?"

"You better lose yourself in the music, the moment — you own it,
You better never let it go. You only get one shot,
Do not miss your chance to blow.
This opportunity comes once in a lifetime."

Chase, this IS that moment. This IS that opportunity. Own it. Be present. Every match. Every period. Every second. Do not leave anything in the tank. Do not walk off that mat wondering "what if." Do not let this slip.

Lose yourself in the moment. Let everything else fade away. The crowd. The pressure. The noise. Let it all disappear until it's just you, your opponent, and the mat. That's your zone. That's your moment. That's YOUR time.


THIS IS YOUR TIME

This is your moment, Chase. This is your time.

You remember the song — Fabolous, "My Time." We played it in the car. You felt it. That was YOUR song. Play it tomorrow. Before you leave. Before you walk into that arena. Play it and feel every word.

"My time's coming soon..."

Your time is NOW, Buddy Boy.

You are the #4 seed at IKWF State. Do you know what that means? That means out of every kid in Illinois — every club, every town, every dream — YOU are ranked 4th. They had to put your name on a seeding chart and say "this kid is one of the best in the entire state."

But seeding is just a number. What matters is who shows up. What matters is who has the heart. What matters is who has been preparing for THIS moment.

And that's you.


THE JOB AIN'T FINISHED

Kobe said it best: "The job ain't finished."

He said it after Game 3 of the 2009 Finals, when the Lakers were up 2-1 and everyone else was celebrating. Kobe looked at the media like they were crazy. "The job ain't finished. What's there to be happy about?"

That's you right now, Buddy Boy. You made it to State. You're the #4 seed. Everyone around you is impressed. Everyone is telling you how proud they are. And you should feel that. Let it in. Absorb it.

Then go to work.

Because the job ain't finished.

You didn't come this far to come this far, Chase. You didn't grind all season, all those early mornings, all those extra practices, all that sacrifice — you didn't do all of that just to show up. You didn't do it just to take a picture in front of the State banner. You did it to WIN.

But here's the truth that matters most: you already won.

The kid who started this season is not the same kid reading this letter. You grew. You evolved. You became a wrestler. A competitor. A young man who stares pressure in the face and doesn't blink. A young man who knows that hard work pays off. That discipline creates freedom. That preparation creates confidence.

No one can ever take that from you. No matter what happens tomorrow. No matter what the scoreboard says. You are different now. You are better now. And you will carry that with you for the rest of your life.

But tomorrow? Tomorrow, go get the trophy too.

Go finish the job. Not for me. Not for Mom. Not for the seeding or the trophy or the medal or the bragging rights.

For YOU.

Because you deserve to see what happens when preparation meets opportunity. Because you deserve to know what it feels like when all the work pays off. Because you put in the time, the sweat, the sacrifice — and you owe it to YOURSELF to leave it all on the mat.


FINAL WORDS

I am proud of you, Chase. I am proud of WHO you are — not just what you do.

You are kind. You are loyal. You love your family. You love Jesus. You work hard. You don't quit. You get back up. Every. Single. Time.

Those things matter more than any trophy. Those things are who you REALLY are.

But tomorrow? Tomorrow, go get the trophy too.

This is your time, Buddy Boy.

"I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13

Your guardian angels flank you. Your Great-Grandma Paulette watches over you. Jesus walks with you. Mom and Dad have your corner. Your whole family is behind you.

Go be great, Chase.

I love you.

— Dad
