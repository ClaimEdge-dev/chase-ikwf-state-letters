A LETTER FOR CHASE — THIS IS YOUR TIME

Chase,

I started writing this mid-season, Buddy Boy. The night I first saw it — really saw it — in your eyes. That look. That fire. That thing that separates kids who wrestle from kids who ARE wrestlers.

I went home that night and started typing. I kept rewriting it, week after week, because every time I thought I had it right, you went and did something else that proved me wrong. You kept leveling up. So I kept telling you instead of finishing the letter. Every car ride. Every practice. Every moment between us — I told you.

And you listened. Not with your ears. With your heart.

Now here we are. The night before State. And I'm finishing this letter because you already lived every word I was trying to write.

THE PRAYER — YOUR TEAM

You are never alone on that mat, Chase. You have a team that no one else can see. Guardian angels — spiritual warriors assigned by God to protect you, to guide you, to give you strength when yours runs out. They flank you on both sides. They have your back. They are God's warriors, and they do not lose.

And right there with them — your Great-Grandma Paulette. She is watching over you, Buddy Boy. She sees you. She knows your heart. She's been praying for this moment since before you were born. When you feel that extra warmth, that calm in the storm — that's her. That's her love wrapping around you like a hug from heaven.

Call upon Jesus. Whisper His name. "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13. That is not just a verse. That is a promise. That is a weapon. Every time you step on that mat, you walk with the King of Kings. The God who created the universe — He chose YOU for this moment. He equipped you. He anointed you.

Mom and Dad are right here. We have bled for this season. We have sacrificed. We have believed when it was hard. And we would do it all again. We are your corner. Always.

All your grandparents helped get you here — Grandma Debbie, Grandpa Bob, Grandma Denise, Grandpa Tommy, Grandma Kim. Every one of them has poured into you. Every one of them will be cheering.

Your brother Jayden looks up to you more than you know. Your sister Nova — she's your biggest little fan. Uncle Bobby, Uncle Brandon, Aunt Kelsey, Aunt Kassidy, Uncle Tyler — this is a family thing, Buddy Boy. This is what we do. We show up for each other.

THE CHAMPIONSHIP MINDSET

Muhammad Ali said: "I am the greatest. I said that even before I knew I was." 

Chase, say it. Believe it. Not from arrogance — from preparation. You are the greatest BECAUSE you have outworked everyone. Your preparation is your confidence. Your preparation is your swagger.

Michael Jordan: "I've missed more than 9,000 shots in my career. I've lost almost 300 games. Twenty-six times I've been trusted to take the game-winning shot and missed. I've failed over and over and over again in my life. And that is why I succeed."

Jordan failed publicly. He failed famously. And he kept shooting. Chase, if you lose a match — if something doesn't go your way — you keep shooting. You keep attacking. You keep believing.

Kobe Bryant: "Everything negative — pressure, challenges — is all an opportunity for me to rise."

Pressure is a privilege, Buddy Boy. It means you're exactly where you're supposed to be.

Tom Brady: "Too often in life, something happens and we blame other people for us not being happy or satisfied or fulfilled. So the point is, we all have choices, and we make the choice to accept people or situations or to not accept situations."

You control your response. You control your effort. You control your attitude. That's it. That's all you need.

Dan Gable: "Once you've wrestled, everything else in life is easy."

Chase, you have already done the hard part. You made it to State. You earned the #4 seed. Now you just go be you.

ROCKY VS DRAGO — YOU KNOW WHO YOU ARE

Remember when we watched Rocky IV? Rocky was the underdog. Drago was the machine — bigger, stronger, "better" on paper. But Rocky had heart. Rocky had something Drago couldn't measure. Something money and science and steroids couldn't buy.

Chase, you are Rocky. The #1 seed, the #2 seed, the #3 seed — they might look like Drago on paper. They might have the bigger name, the bigger club, the bigger reputation. But they don't have YOUR heart. They didn't wake up at 5am with you. They didn't run those sprints. They didn't push through when their body said stop.

You did.

And that makes you dangerous, Buddy Boy. That makes you someone they should be worried about.

EMINEM — LOSE YOURSELF

"You better lose yourself in the music, the moment — you own it, you better never let it go. You only get one shot, do not miss your chance to blow. This opportunity comes once in a lifetime."

Chase, this IS that moment. This IS that opportunity. Own it. Be present. Every match. Every period. Every second. Do not leave anything in the tank. Do not walk off that mat wondering "what if."

THIS IS YOUR TIME

This is your moment, Chase. This is your time. The Fabolous song — remember? "My Time." Play it. Feel it. This is YOUR season. YOUR tournament. YOUR story.

You are the #4 seed at IKWF State. Do you know what that means? That means out of every kid in Illinois — every club, every town, every dream — YOU are ranked 4th. They had to put your name on a seeding chart and say "this kid is one of the best."

But seeding is just a number, Buddy Boy. What matters is who shows up. What matters is who has the heart. What matters is who has been preparing for THIS moment.

And that's you.

THE JOB AIN'T FINISHED

Kobe said it best: "The job ain't finished." 

You didn't come this far to come this far, Chase. You didn't grind all season, all those early mornings, all those extra practices, all that sacrifice — you didn't do all of that just to show up. You didn't do it just to take a picture in front of the State banner. You did it to WIN.

But here's the truth: you already won. The kid who started this season is not the same kid reading this letter. You grew. You evolved. You became a wrestler. A competitor. A young man who stares pressure in the face and smiles.

So go finish the job. Not for me. Not for Mom. Not for the seeding or the trophy or the medal.

For YOU.

Because you deserve to see what happens when preparation meets opportunity.

FINAL WORDS

I am proud of you, Chase. I am proud of WHO you are — not just what you do. You are kind. You are loyal. You love your family. You love Jesus. You work hard. You don't quit.

Those things matter more than any trophy. But tomorrow? Tomorrow, go get the trophy too.

This is your time.

I love you, Buddy Boy.

— Dad
