THE JOB AIN'T FINISHED

Chase,

"The job ain't finished." — Kobe Bryant

He said it after Game 3 of the 2009 Finals, when his team was up 2-1 and everyone else was celebrating. Kobe looked at the camera like everyone was crazy and said, "The job ain't finished. What's there to be happy about?"

That's you right now, Buddy Boy. You made it to State. You're the #4 seed. Everyone around you is impressed. Everyone is patting you on the back. And you should feel proud — for a minute. Then you get back to work.

Because the job ain't finished.

You don't get a seed. You get a title. You get a memory that lasts forever. You get to walk into that middle school next year — and high school after that — and know that you are a STATE QUALIFIER. A STATE PLACER! A STATE CHAMPION!

But that's not given. That's earned. Right here. Right now.

Every kid you wrestle wants it just as bad as you. Every single one of them has a dad writing them a letter. Every single one of them has a dream. 

So what makes you different?

Your heart. Your preparation. Your team — seen and unseen. Your Great-Grandma Paulette watching over you. Your guardian angels flanking you. Jesus walking with you.

"I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me." Say it. Believe it. Live it.

Mom and Dad are right here. We have bled for this. We have sacrificed. We have believed. All your grandparents — Grandma Debbie, Grandpa Bob, Grandma Denise, Grandpa Tommy, Grandma Kim — they all poured into you. Jayden, Nova, your aunts and uncles — we're all here.

Go finish the job, Chase. I love you.

— Dad
